# Risk and Status

## Status Reporting

Use Green, Yellow, and Red honestly:

- Green: On track, no significant unresolved risks.
- Yellow: At risk, mitigation is underway, outcome is uncertain.
- Red: Off track, help or a major decision is needed.

Move to Yellow at the first meaningful risk. Move to Red when the current plan cannot meet the commitment without intervention.

## Risk Communication

For material risks, state:

1. What may happen and why.
2. The impact if it happens.
3. The likelihood and evidence.
4. The mitigation plan and owner.
5. The specific ask, if help is needed.

Avoid vague risk language. Replace "there may be delays" with what may slip, by how much, why, and what decision or mitigation is needed.

## Risk Quality Bar

- Lead with major risks instead of burying them after progress.
- Do not list risks without mitigation unless the point is to escalate that no mitigation exists.
- Separate risk from issue: a risk may happen; an issue is already happening.
- Make ownership explicit.
- Make the ask actionable.

## ROAM Labels

Use ROAM when helpful:

- Resolved: no longer a concern; note how it was resolved.
- Owned: acknowledged and actively managed; name the owner and mitigation.
- Accepted: known but intentionally not mitigated; document why.
- Mitigated: reduced to acceptable level; state what changed.
